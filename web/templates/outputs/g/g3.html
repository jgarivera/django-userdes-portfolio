What is Design and User Design?

Design is the process of arranging and structuring elements to form a pattern or composition. User design is the process of designing the structure and elements of a product for the sole user.

What is the difference between User Experience Design and User Interface Design? Why does it matter?

User experience (UX) design is about designing products that are user-friendly. Furthermore, it is all about carefully designing and providing functional interaction and experiences for the users. UX design is attributed to overall feel of the experience (Lamprecht, 2019). On the other hand, user interface (UI) design is the process of designing the look and function of a product’s interface. UI design is all about visuals and graphical elements such as buttons, icons, colors schemes, spacing, fonts, responsiveness and much more. UX and UI design goes hand-in-hand and closely integrates with one another. They both matter hence both UX and UI design are integral in developing and producing a successful product (Lamprecht, 2019). If the users find the product’s UX and UI intuitive and functional, they are guaranteed to like and keep using the product thus lead to the product’s success.

Who are the users? Why are they important?

The users are the ones that actually use and interact with a particular product. They are important because they are the sole reason why a product exists. Great UI and UX leads to user loyalty and satisfaction for the product which leads to its success (Lamprecht, 2019). Moreover, a product exists for the users themselves and that is why it is important to create a user-centered product.
