  G3: What is Prototyping?

What is prototyping?

Prototyping is an experimental process. It is where designers turn their ideas from paper to something more tangible (Interaction Design Foundation, 2020). It is also iterative. One can keep refining, reconceptualizing and validating designs until it is the most appropriate and correct. With this, designers could definitely create and provide the right products to the end-users.

What is the difference between low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes?

Fidelity refers to the level of detail and functionality that a prototype manifest (Interaction Design Foundation, 2020). Low-fidelity prototypes, such as paper prototypes, merely provide a quick overall look of the intended design. It is fast and cheap to produce. It is also disposable, easy to iterate from and make changes with. Although fast and cheap, the lack of specific details makes it difficult for the users to provide feedback with it (Interaction Design Foundation, 2020). Since low-fidelity often suggest ambiguous design, it is far off from realism and requires a lot of imagination for the user to attain a concrete perspective.

On the other hand, high-fidelity prototypes, such as digital prototypes created with image editing software, are grander and engaging (Interaction Design Foundation, 2020). The vision of the final product is more clear-cut and apparent in this kind of prototype. Users could judge and understand more what the planned design is. With this, they could provide more critical and accurate feedback. Although grander and engaging, it is costlier, and more time is needed to craft a prototype with high-fidelity (Interaction Design Foundation, 2020).

Why is prototyping important?

Prototyping is important because it allows designers to experiment and communicate intended features. In this stage, they could come up with something tangible and also something that is critically measurable. They could hand out these prototypes to early users so that they could garner immediate feedback about their early designs before it gets too costly or too complicated to revert back. Prototyping is seemingly analogous to the quote: “prevention is better than the cure.” Prototyping allows us to see and evaluate designs that would not work; based on the feedback from the users. We prevent unwanted costs in the long run.
