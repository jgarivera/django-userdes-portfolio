  R6: Color Theory and Typography

Color and theory and typography are two close topics to me. They al pertain to visuals. Whenever I think of visuals, I immediately get the idea of showing texts, images, shapes and colors. The elements that form what we see in our daily lives. Thankfully, I got to learn more about color theory and typography interactively with fun games in our USERDES class. Some were straightforward. Some were challenging and confusing. All in all, it was a fun experience to have played these fun and educational games. I got to learn more about colors and fonts in depth and greater detail.

The first game that I played was the Kern Type kerning game. It is about tweaking the perfect horizontal space between letters among different font styles. I had an intuition in attempting the game. I just went with my gut feeling. If it felt “right,” then it probably is right! Getting a perfect score of one hundred is oddly satisfying. I managed to achieve that after multiple tries. The game did teach me about how intricate font styles are. They are definitely well-thought of. The horizontal spacing that we usually overlook everyday in our screen fonts is something to think about. It gives me a window to appreciate all these kinds of things! The second game that I played was the Brandseen coloring game. This was the game I had so much confidence in playing. I know and recognize brand colors well. However, when I begun to play and guess the colors, it was a slight bit difficult to answer. Like for instance, I know that the Coca-Cola brand is color red. Yes, but which kind of red? I followed my intuition once again and for most of the time, I managed to get most brand colors accurate! Another mini achievement indeed! The third game was certainly the toughest among the typography games I played. It is the ever-so-daunting Better Web Type typography game! Honestly, the patterns were confusing, and I just followed my instincts yet again. Some time it worked and some time it did not work. My girlfriend and I remarked the game’s triangle as the “dreaded triangle.” It is occasionally hard to get a perfect score for each section! I learned more about the intricacies of font styles. They possess font size, line length and line height. It really shows that they are significantly two-dimensional. The last game that I played was the Type War typography game. I find it relaxing to play actually. It was more of a zen game. It was about guessing what font style a certain letter is. I was quite familiar with a number of fonts. The clue that I always had in mind was whether the displayed letter is in serif or sans serif. This clue is big enough to uncover the details for me.

I enjoyed playing all these fun color theory and typography games. I got the chance to learn these topics in an interactive and memorable way.
